The opportunity

Byon does not need another KPI file. It needs a system that makes the KPIs work.

Your existing KPI draft already defines meaningful measures across departments. The next challenge is turning those measures into a repeatable management rhythm.

01

Goals can get lost between departments

Byon has interdependent teams across revenue, operations, customer service, finance, marketing and support. Without one operating rhythm, priorities can become departmental activities instead of shared business results.

02

Management visibility arrives too late

A KPI workbook is a useful start, but management still needs reliable data ownership, review frequency and clear follow-up when a metric slips.

03

Rewards can feel subjective

If recognition or incentives are introduced before role definitions and data are trusted, employees may question fairness instead of feeling motivated.

What Vimigo changes

From “management chasing” to a performance-driven operating rhythm.

Implementation roadmap

A 30-day management implementation — not a software setup exercise.

1

Phase 0

Diagnose and align

Confirm the 90-day business goal, pilot departments, role owners, baseline data and current approval rules.
2

Week 1

Build the foundation

Set up roles, access, daily operating workflows and manager ownership. Resolve data gaps before scoring people.
3

Week 2

Stabilise daily use

Review exceptions, improve adoption and make sure the information is reliable enough for management decisions.
4

Week 3

Connect goals to KPIs

Configure role-specific goals for Operations, Customer Service and Revenue, then begin structured weekly reviews.
5

Week 4

Review and decide

Deliver an adoption and data-quality review, refine targets and decide whether rewards or leaderboards are ready.
Important: Cash rewards, negative scoring and leaderboards should not launch until management, Finance and department owners approve the rules and the underlying data is reliable.
